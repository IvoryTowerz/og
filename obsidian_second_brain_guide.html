The Problem

When you follow the standard Second Brain setup, Obsidian graphs everything — templates, attachments, AI helper files, system notes. The result is a cloud of infrastructure noise instead of a network of connected thinking.

Most setups today
  • Notes = storage + AI dumping ground
  • Graph = messy cloud of system files
  • You see your infrastructure, not your mind
What you actually want
  • Notes = thinking units
  • Graph = decision network
  • Clusters of ideas, bridges of insight
The guide makes it sound automatic. It's not. Your graph becomes useful only when you write real notes, link ideas intentionally, and build relationships over time. It gets sharper every single day you use it — but the work is real.

Part 1 Fix the graph now

Four steps to go from a messy cloud to a clean, high-signal knowledge graph.

1

Basic noise filter

Paste this into Graph View → ⚙️ to immediately remove most of the clutter:

-path:_Templates -path:_Attachments -path:.obsidian
2

Elite include-only filter (recommended)

Go further — show only meaningful nodes, nothing else:

path:Projects OR path:People OR path:Resources OR path:Areas
3

Kill the visual clutter

Inside Graph settings:

  • Turn off: Attachments, Orphans (optional)
  • Set Link Distance: 1–2
4

Start linking — this is what people skip

Even with filters, your graph looks empty unless notes are actually connected. Three directions, always:

  • [[Big Idea]] — link up to concepts and strategy
  • [[Project X]] — link down to execution
  • [[Person Name]] — link sideways to people and resources

No links = no graph. This is the network effect the whole system depends on.

After 30–50 notes with proper linking, you'll start to see what the graph is actually for: tight clusters representing business domains, bridge nodes representing strategic insights, people connected to the projects they affect.

Part 2 Executive folder structure

Keep it intentional and simple. Each folder has exactly one job.

FolderPurpose
00_System/Dashboards, Index pages, Maps of Content — filter this out of the main graph
10_Areas/Ongoing responsibilities (Health, Finance, Business Ops, Marketing)
20_Projects/Finite outcomes with clear goals and end dates
30_Knowledge/Your intellectual capital — this is where the real graph magic lives
40_People/Powerful relationship mapping — linked to projects and knowledge
50_Journal/Daily capture — optional, keep out of main graph
90_Archive/Completed items — filter out to reduce noise
_Templates/ + _Assets/Garbage layer — never in graph, ever
Master graph filter — paste this now
-path:_Templates -path:_Assets -path:00_System -path:50_Journal -path:90_Archive
Combined with elite include-only layer
(path:20_Projects OR path:30_Knowledge OR path:40_People OR path:10_Areas)

Together, these give you only meaningful nodes, no system noise, and clear clusters.

Part 3 Build notes that actually think

Folder structure means nothing if your notes are dead storage. The real upgrade is making every note in 30_Knowledge/ a connected thinking unit.

Pro note structure — use this template in 30_Knowledge/
# AI Strategy

## Related
- [[Sales Systems]]
- [[Client Acquisition]]
- [[Automation]]

## Used In
- [[Project X]]
- [[Launch Consulting Offer]]

## People
- [[John Smith]]

## Notes
Your actual thinking here.

This forces connections, creates clustering, and builds graph intelligence over time. The linking strategy is everything:

  • Link UP to big ideas → [[AI Strategy]] [[Growth Loops]]
  • Link DOWN to execution → [[Project X]]
  • Link SIDEWAYS to people and resources → [[Client ABC]] [[Book: $100M Offers]]

Part 6 Turn your vault into a decision engine

This is the step most people never reach. Right now your vault is probably a storage and AI dumping ground. Here's how you convert it into a decision engine and leverage system.

Your system now does something powerful: it connects thinking → action → outcome. That feedback loop is the whole game.

Step C — Force every project to link to knowledge

Open each project note in 20_Projects/ and add a section:

## Driven By
- [[AI Strategy]]
- [[Sales Systems]]

## Key Decisions
- [[Decision: Launch Strategy]]

Now projects aren't isolated notes — they're tied to the strategy that drives them and the decisions that shaped them.

Step D — Build feedback loops

This is what makes the system elite. Every time something happens in the real world:

Experience
→
Update Project (outcome)
→
Refine Knowledge note
→
Update Decision note (result)
→
Better decisions next time

Example: you try a sales tactic. It works. You update the Project (outcome logged), refine the Knowledge note with the new insight, and update the Decision note with what actually happened. Now your vault is a system that learns from your experience — automatically, because you built the structure to capture it.

The real shift: Experience gets converted into refined insight, which drives better decisions, which drives better outcomes. This is the compounding effect everyone talks about but few actually build.

Part 7 AI as chief of staff

Turn Claude or ChatGPT into a systems operator for your vault. These are the battle-tested prompts — not "help me write notes," but "operate my knowledge system."

Prompt 1 — Master prompt (set once, use always)
You are my Chief of Staff and knowledge architect. I use Obsidian as a second brain with this structure: 00_System/ | 10_Areas/ | 20_Projects/ | 30_Knowledge/ | 40_People/ | 50_Journal/ | 90_Archive/ Your job is to: - Turn raw ideas into structured notes - Ensure every note links to related knowledge, projects, and people - Help me make better decisions (not just store information) - Identify gaps, inefficiencies, and opportunities in my thinking - Suggest connections between ideas automatically When creating notes: - Always include internal links [[like this]] - Always connect to strategy (knowledge), execution (projects), and people when relevant - Keep notes concise, actionable, and decision-oriented When I give you input — decide whether it should be: 1. A Knowledge note 2. A Project update 3. A Decision note 4. A Person note Then create or update it properly. Optimize for: → clarity → leverage → decision quality → speed of execution
Prompt 2 — Turn any idea into a proper note
Convert the following into a properly structured Obsidian note using my system. - Place it in the correct folder - Add internal links - Connect it to strategy, projects, and people - Make it useful for decisions, not just storage Input: [PASTE IDEA HERE]
Prompt 3 — Decision notes
Turn this situation into a Decision note. Include: - context - options - tradeoffs - linked knowledge - impacted projects and people - a recommended decision Make it sharp and executive-level. Situation: [PASTE PROBLEM HERE]
Prompt 4 — Force connections (fixes your graph)
Analyze this note and suggest: 1. Related knowledge notes 2. Related projects 3. Relevant people 4. Missing links that would improve decision-making Then rewrite the note with those connections added using Obsidian [[links]]. Note: [PASTE NOTE]
Prompt 5 — Weekly CEO review
Act as my CEO advisor. Based on the following projects, decisions, and current priorities: Identify: 1. What matters most right now 2. What I should stop doing 3. Where I'm making poor tradeoffs 4. The highest leverage move for next week Then update my CEO Dashboard. Data: [PASTE DASHBOARD OR NOTES]
Prompt 6 — Organize your vault automatically (use with Claude Code / Desktop)
Scan my Obsidian vault and: 1. Move notes into the correct folders: - Knowledge → 30_Knowledge - Projects → 20_Projects - People → 40_People 2. Add internal links between related notes 3. Identify orphan notes and connect them 4. Suggest missing Decision notes where decisions are implied but not captured 5. Create or update my: - CEO Dashboard - Master Knowledge Index Do not duplicate notes — improve structure instead.
Prompt 7 — Turn your vault into a strategy engine
Analyze my notes as a system. Identify: - patterns in my thinking - repeated problems or bottlenecks - underutilized knowledge - opportunities for leverage Then: - recommend 3 strategic shifts - suggest new knowledge notes I should create - suggest decisions I should be making right now Focus on increasing: revenue, clarity, execution speed.
Pro tip: When using Claude Desktop with your vault connected, you're not asking it to "help you write notes." You're asking it to operate your knowledge system like a Chief of Staff. That framing changes everything about how you use it.

The Daily Loop

Simple. Five steps. Run this every day and the system compounds.

  1. 1
    Open CEO Dashboard — work from Top Priorities only
  2. 2
    Dump new ideas → use Prompt 2 to structure them properly
  3. 3
    When stuck on a decision → create a Decision note via Prompt 3
  4. 4
    Link everything you touch — up, down, and sideways
  5. 5
    End of day → update outcomes on active projects

Weekly: run Prompt 5 (CEO Review). Occasionally: run Prompt 6 (organize vault) and Prompt 7 (strategy analysis).
